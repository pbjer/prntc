I started building [Ouijit](https://github.com/ouijit/ouijit) in a fit of hype right after reading Steve Yegge's [*Gas Town*](https://steve-yegge.medium.com/welcome-to-gas-town-4f25ee16dd04) in January. V1 of Ouijit was birthed in a couple hours, and it was actually all about sharing and running software as opposed to making it. I have since horseshoed back to thinking thats a more interesting thing to work on… but the result of this detour is a fun way to manage a bunch of terminals (mainly agent terminals, but it’s no requirement), and therefore the production of software these days.

Projects in Ouijit get their own kanban board of tasks, and those tasks can have any number of terminals connected to them. No innovative UX here, just an ergonomic way to manage the macro of agent work (and combat the disorganization I often find myself in). What’s more interesting  to me is preserving the general terminal experience, and having some layout options that aren’t what I’ll dub as “the hackerman t-mux grid”.
